The many types of meditation

Meditation is a technique to train attention and awareness,
achieve mental clarity, and reach an emotionally calm, stable state.

Common meditation techniques

  • Breathing: Focus on your breath, feeling and listening to it as you inhale and exhale. You can also try saying "breathe in" and "breathe out" in your head as you do.
  • Body scan: Focus on each part of your body, noticing how it feels.
  • Mantra: Repeat a mantra, which can be religious or not.
  • Walking meditation: Meditate while walking, anywhere you are.
  • Prayer: Pray, which is a well-known type of meditation.
  • Reading and reflecting: Read poems or sacred texts and think about their meaning.
  • Listening to music: Listen to sacred music, spoken words, or any music that relaxes or inspires you.
  • Focused meditation: Focus on something with intention, such as a visual, auditory, or constant thing.
  • Progressive muscle relaxation: Start at one end of your body and work through it, tensing and relaxing muscles, or visualising a wave drifting over your body.
